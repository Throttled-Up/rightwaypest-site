<ServiceSection slot="section_1" heading="Why Jacksonville Homes Face Year-Round Pest Pressure">

Jacksonville's subtropical humidity creates conditions where pest populations never fully cycle down. Duval and St. Johns County's sandy soils offer almost no natural resistance to subterranean entry. Older neighborhoods like Arlington, Riverside, and San Marco compound the problem with pier-and-beam foundations that give pests direct access beneath the structure.

The pressure shifts by season but never stops. German cockroaches and palmetto bugs surge through summer. Fire ants colonize sandy yards from spring through fall. Mild winters push rodents indoors rather than driving them into dormancy. Humid crawl spaces sustain silverfish colonies twelve months a year. Florida's Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services flags Northeast Florida as a high-pressure zone for wood-destroying organisms, meaning termite risk runs alongside everything else.

A generic quarterly spray applied on a calendar schedule cannot account for these overlapping pressures. Jacksonville's climate produces specific pest profiles that shift month to month, and treatment protocols must track those shifts, not ignore them.

<ServiceSection slot="section_3" heading="What We Treat in Northeast Florida Homes">

Jacksonville's warm, humid environment and proximity to waterways create ideal conditions for a wide range of household pests. Here's what we treat and how.

**Crawling insects:** German cockroaches, American cockroaches (palmetto bugs), silverfish, earwigs, and spiders, including brown recluse and black widow species we identify during inspection. We use targeted gel baits for cockroach colonies, placing them in harborage zones where these pests breed.

**Stinging and biting pests:** Fire ants (*Solenopsis invicta*) are a serious liability in Jacksonville yards, especially with children and pets. We treat colonies with mound-drench applications or broadcast bait depending on colony scope. Wasps and yellow jackets receive direct nest treatment.

**Rodents:** Roof rats are common in older neighborhoods like Ortega and Murray Hill, while Norway rats appear frequently near waterfront properties. We deploy tamper-resistant bait stations along active runways.

**Occasional invaders:** Millipedes, centipedes, and Asian lady beetles addressed through perimeter barrier treatments.

For termite and mosquito concerns, we offer dedicated control programs separate from the bi-monthly barrier plan.
