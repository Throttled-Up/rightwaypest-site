<ServiceSection slot="section_2" heading="Ant Species We Target in Northeast Florida Properties">

Northeast Florida properties deal with five ant species that each demand a different control strategy.

**Carpenter ants** (*Camponotus floridanus* and *C. tortuganus*) don't consume wood. They excavate galleries through moisture-damaged fascia boards, window frames, and bath traps, creating structural damage that mimics termite activity.

**Red imported fire ants** (*Solenopsis invicta*) thrive in St. Augustine turf across Duval and St. Johns Counties. Newer developments like Nocatee and Bartram Park see especially dense populations where disturbed soils give colonies easy establishment.

**Ghost ants** (*Tapinoma melanocephalum*) are barely visible, translucent-bodied, and the most common kitchen invaders in Jacksonville homes. They trail along countertops and plumbing lines.

**White-footed ants** form supercolonies with multiple queens. Store-bought baits rarely make a dent because the colony simply redistributes.

**Bigheaded ants** are displacing fire ants in several Duval County neighborhoods, pushing up soil mounds that disrupt turf root systems and irrigation lines.

Each species requires a targeted protocol built around its biology and nesting behavior.

<ServiceSection slot="section_7" heading="DIY Ant Products vs. Professional Ant Control: What Actually Works">

That can of pyrethroid spray from the hardware store doesn't just fail against ghost ants and white-footed ants. It actively makes the problem worse. These species respond to repellent chemicals by budding: the colony fragments, queens scatter, and what started as one colony behind your kitchen backsplash becomes four satellite colonies spread across the house.

Big-box bait stations create a similar dead end because the bait matrix rarely matches the species' current dietary phase. Ghost ants cycle between protein and carbohydrate preferences, so a mismatched bait just gets ignored.

We use non-repellent transfer products that foragers pick up unknowingly and carry back to the queen, collapsing the colony from the inside. For fire ant mounds in Jacksonville yards, broadcast bait applications work effectively. Structural ants inside your home demand species-matched professional treatment.

Some DIY measures genuinely help. Sealing entry points, fixing moisture sources, and trimming vegetation away from the structure are all steps we recommend as part of our integrated pest management approach.
