<ServiceSection slot="section_2" heading="Our Annual Fertilization & Weed Control Program for St. Augustine Grass">

Jacksonville's sandy soils and subtropical humidity demand a precise annual calendar for St. Augustine turf. Here's how our rounds break down across the year:

- **Late January:** Pre-emergent application to block crabgrass and annual sedge before germination.
- **March:** Slow-release granular fertilizer with iron and manganese for spring green-up without phosphorus runoff.
- **May:** Liquid broadleaf treatment targeting dollarweed, clover, and matchweed using selective herbicides safe for St. Augustine.
- **Summer (June through September):** Micronutrient applications, including iron and manganese, during Duval County's nitrogen blackout period. Potassium applications maintain root health without violating the ordinance.
- **Fall:** Potassium-heavy formula for root hardening before winter stress.
- **Late Fall:** Pre-emergent for Poa annua suppression heading into the cooler months.

We use polymer-coated and sulfur-coated urea as slow-release nitrogen sources specifically because they resist leaching in sandy Northeast Florida soils. Every granular application runs through calibrated spreaders. Liquid treatments go out via backpack sprayers for precise coverage. Visible density improvement arrives within four to six weeks. Full program results show by the second annual cycle.
